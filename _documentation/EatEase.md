C.	Technology
EatEase provides a solid foundation of technologies that will be used for the development of the entire system. The backend infrastructure is built on Python and Flask, ensuring a robust and scalable environment and the development will be done in PyCharm as its integrated development environment (IDE), which will be used for coding and debugging. As for the web application functionalities, EatEase will utilize Hostinger which provides a dedicated domain name, and the resources needed on hosting the website. For the data storage and management of the system, it will use PostgreSQL for its database management system (DBMS). Additionally, EatEase will invest in a cloud computing service from Amazon Web Services (AWS), which can provide reliable data storage, management, and security.

The EatEase application makes meal preparation easier by providing personalized recipe suggestions based on the ingredients users have at home. Users can input their available ingredients either by typing them in manually or by using the app's camera feature, which uses computer vision to identify ingredients in real-time. Additionally, users can specify their dietary preferences, such as vegan or gluten-free, and indicate the type of meal they want to prepare, like breakfast, lunch, or dinner. 
Once the ingredients and preferences are entered, the app processes this information by detecting any camera input and storing the data securely. The AI then analyzes the ingredients and preferences to generate recipe suggestions. It matches available ingredients with recipes in its database, filters out options that do not meet dietary restrictions, and ranks the recipes based on factors like preparation time and nutritional value.
The app presents users with a list of recipes they can make, including the recipe name, required ingredients (highlighting what they already have), cooking instructions, and nutritional information. After trying the recipes, users can provide feedback, which helps the AI improve future suggestions. Overall, EatEase aims to make cooking enjoyable and accessible for everyone.

g.	Market Research
 
The cooking habits of 33 respondents aged 18 to 40 years were surveyed via Google Forms to determine the potential demand for the product before it would be rolled out. The majority (45.5%) cook rarely (1-2 times a week), followed by 27.3% who cook often (5-7 times a week), 18.2% who cook sometimes (3-4 times a week), and 9.1% who never cook at home. While direct feedback on the product's taste was not feasible through this survey, EatEase considered insights from respondents in General Santos City, focusing on aspects such as Pricing, Psychological Factors, and Cultural and Social Factors.

Pricing

The survey results indicate a strong preference for subscription-based pricing models for an AI-powered meal planner with personalized features. A significant portion of respondents (38.4%) are willing to pay a freemium subscription fee per month for a comprehensive plan. Additionally, a considerable number (51.5%) are open to a free subscription. Interestingly, there's also interest in a discounted annual plan, suggesting a potential strategy to attract long-term subscribers. To entice initial users, a free version or freemium model with basic features could be effective. This approach could allow users to experience the product's value before committing to a paid subscription.


Psychological Factors


The survey results reveal that the most significant challenge faced by respondents when meal planning is sticking to a budget, followed closely by finding time to plan and dealing with busy schedules. A considerable number also struggle with choosing balanced or healthy meals and managing ingredients. These findings underscore the need for meal planning tools and services that can help users save time, money, and make healthier food choices. By addressing these common challenges, such tools can significantly improve people's meal planning experience.
 



Cultural and Social Factors

The survey results indicate that a significant majority of respondents (66.7%) do not regularly cook meals inspired by their cultural background. Only 33.3% of respondents reported regularly preparing culturally-inspired dishes. This suggests that while cultural heritage is valued, it may not be a primary influence on daily cooking habits.

Chapter VI
POTENTIAL RISKS AND PROBLEMS

This chapter discusses the different potential risks and problems that the business may encounter during the operations.
While starting any new business always entails some element of risk, there are plenty of ways that both current and future entrepreneurs may be able to protect themselves better. EatEase identified the following potential risks and problems that it may encounter as the business progress:
1.	Market Competition
The meal planning app market is becoming increasingly saturated, with numerous competitors offering similar services. This could lead to price wars and reduced market share. To mitigate this risk, EatEase will focus on differentiating its offerings through unique features, superior user experience, and targeted marketing strategies.
2.	Technology Risk
Issues related to app performance, data security, and the accuracy of computer vision technology may affect user experience. Any technical failures could lead to user dissatisfaction and loss of trust. To address this, EatEase will invest in robust testing, regular updates, and cybersecurity measures to protect user data.
3.	Regulation Compliance
Adhering to food safety regulations and data protection laws (such as GDPR) is crucial for EatEase to avoid legal complications. Non-compliance could result in fines and damage to the brand's reputation. EatEase will stay informed about relevant regulations and implement necessary compliance measures from the outset.


4.	User Adaption
Attracting and retaining users can be challenging, especially in a crowded market. If users do not find the EatEase app valuable or user-friendly, they may abandon it. To combat this, EatEase will conduct user research, gather feedback, and continuously improve the app based on user needs and preferences.
5.	Financial Risk
Fluctuations in funding and revenue generation could impact the operational sustainability of EatEase. If initial funding does not cover operational costs or if revenue growth is slower than anticipated, the business may face cash flow issues. EatEase will develop a detailed financial plan, including budgeting and forecasting, to manage expenses and seek additional funding sources if necessary.
6.	Changing Consumer Preferences
Shifts in dietary trends and consumer preferences can impact the relevance of EatEase's offerings. To stay ahead, EatEase will monitor market trends and adapt its features and recommendations accordingly.
